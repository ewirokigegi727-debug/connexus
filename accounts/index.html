Deposits and checking

Accounts designed for everyday use and stronger balances over time

Connexus public product information shows a deposit lineup that goes well beyond a basic checking account. Members can choose checking built for simple fee-free use or pursue higher-yield tiers when they meet qualifying activity. Savings products support emergency funds and short-term goals, while certificates provide a more disciplined option for members who want a fixed term and predictable growth. Money market accounts add flexibility for larger balances, and health savings accounts help qualified members set money aside for medical expenses with tax advantages. The combination gives households a practical way to separate spending cash from growth-oriented reserves without leaving the same digital banking environment.

Health and retirement

Purpose-built savings for medical costs and long-range planning

Specialized accounts are central to the Connexus offering because members often need more than one savings strategy at the same time. HSAs can support qualified healthcare expenses, while IRA options help members prepare for retirement with tax-advantaged structures. These products work best when paired with consistent contributions and regular review, and Connexus complements them with digital visibility plus access to investment-related guidance. For members balancing current obligations with future milestones, that structure helps convert general saving intent into named, trackable goals.

Cards, investment, and business

Support for spending power, planning, and commercial needs

Connexus also expands beyond classic deposit products with credit cards, investment services, and account paths for both individuals and businesses. Credit cards can simplify purchasing and balance management, while investment services give members a way to bring retirement and wealth conversations into the same relationship as everyday banking. Business members benefit from banking tools and financing support that can help with cash handling, operations, and growth planning. Together, these options make the account category more than a place to store money; it becomes the operating center for personal and business financial decisions.
